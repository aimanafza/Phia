01

Price Range Filter

Airbnb-style budget filtering for targeted shopping

+

The Problem

Currently, Phia only offers "Sort: High to Low" and "Sort: Low to High" options. If I have a $100 budget, I still have to scroll through $500+ items. There's no way to filter by price range, making budget-conscious shopping frustrating.

User Need

"Show me only items I can actually afford within my budget"

The Solution

Add an interactive price range slider (Airbnb-style) with:

  • Min/Max price inputs with drag-to-adjust slider
  • Quick-select buttons: "Under $50", "$50-$100", "$100-$200", "$200+"
  • Live result count as users adjust the range
  • Persistent filter state across browsing sessions

"I need to understand what filters mean and easily change my filtering choices"

03

Price History Graph

Google Flights-style pricing insights for smart buying

+

The Problem

Users don't know if now is a good time to buy or if they should wait for a price drop. Without historical context, they might overpay or miss a good deal. This creates hesitation and lost conversions.

User Need

"When's the best time to buy this item? Am I getting a good deal right now?"

The Solution

Add visual price history similar to Google Flights:

  • 30/60/90-day price graph on item pages
  • Clear visualization: "This was $280 last month, now $252"
  • Badge indicators: "‚Üì Best price in 30 days!" or "‚Üë Price increasing"
  • Optional: Email alerts when saved items drop in price
  • Toggle between new and secondhand price histories
